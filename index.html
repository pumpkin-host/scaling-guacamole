What is Sustainable Strategies?

Sustainable Strategies is About Having Knowledge

Sustainable Strategies invites basic strategies to the forefront of the knowledge base to aid in increasing overall sustainability. Sustainability is dynamic and can have different meanings to different people but in the sense of Sustainable Strategies, it is easiest to think of sustainability in terms of stewardship of communities and of the environment. When knowledge is available action is more likely to be taken. Those basic strategies include the broad factors necessary to increase sustainability. When undertaken by enough participants, strategies such as being mindful of reducing carbon emissions, water usage, water pollution and non-biodegradable/non-compostable trash can have a tremendous positive impact on animal health and safety, environmental health and ecosystem flourishment which is vital for future generations to live happy and fulfilled lives and can also help sustainable local farmers with better weather conditions to produce healthier crops which are healthier for people to consume and good for society.

How Does It Work?

okco2.com helps increase the sustainability of decision-making by providing key strategies to focus on for a step in the right direction.

Knowing is the first step

Being an okco2 member and showing the Sustainable Strategies Logo© shows belief in achieving the goals outlined below and is taking a strong interest in exploring the possibilities of increasing sustainability across the key strategic points of interest through better future decision-making. Change is difficult and by knowing the key strategies to increase sustainability it's more likely that some of those strategies will be at the front of the mind during decision-making, to increase the potential sustainability of each decision. That is the ethos of Sustainable Strategies.

What are the Sustainable Strategies?

  • Reducing carbon emissons in scope
  • Reducing water usage
  • Reducing water pollution
  • Reducing non-biodegradable/non-compostable trash
  • Increasing wildlife and ecosystem convservation
  • Increasing local sourcing of purchases
  • Increasing local farming
  • Not using harmful chemicals
  • Caretaking for future generations
